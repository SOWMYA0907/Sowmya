our most insulated jecket to date, the city-style highline is designed to keep you warm during the deep fgreeze of winter.

with 300 grams of primaLoft one insulation, the highline offers incredible and retention while remaining form fiting and low valume--essentially all the warmth of a puffer jacket without all the puff. its outer shell is made from a water-resistant schoeller microfiber fabeic that has been diagonally quited,while its inside liner is made from a silky-soft mircofiber ripstop nylon.

the highline features a zipper garage to prevent a cold metal zipper from pressing against your neck, non-quited side panels and underaram for a sleeker fit, and anchored botton-hem adjecters for a snug fit. it has two uniquely designed 3-in-1 front pockets. which include a top-zippered entrance, a drop pocket with donut snap, and a side hand-warmer. additional storage can be found in its exterior chest pocket and two interior zippered pockets.

THis Thing is a monster! 08/20/2013
☆☆☆☆☆

I picked up ths jacket for use in San Francisco and the surrounding Bay Area (Lake Tahoe included). I use this jacket for everything. It is often cloudy/drizzly/cold in SF and this jacket deals with it all. I have not tried in heavy rain, but in deizzly conditions, the DWR treatment definitely makes small drops bead right off. It also has a nice fleece-like liner inside and keeps me really warm down to 40 degrees or so. In Tahoe , I have used this with a shirt or base layer, mid later fleece, and then this jacket. I don't get cold too easily but inn temps 20's - 30's I felt great (with some gloves and a beanie of course). I have not tried to skiing yet because I snowboard and to be honest, it wouldn't be quiet large enough ( i like baggier snowboard clothing). But I have seen people who wear softshells skiing all the time. I also use this when i go to work and want a jacket tat dresses up nice. With slacks, nice shoes, and button up it looks great. I am 6' and about 190lbs (34 in arms) and the large fits perfect. The pockets are will designed as well. The soft shell is the new "do-everything" jacket, wind water resistent but also maleable and comfy for wearing anywhere. I highly recommend this jacket.
